Slitherlink Solving Lemmas

A structured reference for logical deductions in Slitherlink (スリザーリンク) puzzles

Rules of Slitherlink

  • The grid is made of dots (vertices) connected by potential edges.
  • Draw a single continuous loop along the grid edges.
  • The loop cannot cross or branch — every vertex on the loop has exactly 2 edges.
  • Cells may contain a clue number (0–3) indicating how many of their 4 edges are part of the loop.
  • Cells without a number have no constraint on how many edges are used.

See axioms below for basic operations.

LEMMA 1

Exposed corner of a three

If a corner vertex of a 3-cell has no edges other than the two belonging to the cell (because the others are eliminated or don't exist), both edges at that corner are forced. If they were both absent, only 2 edges would remain — not enough to satisfy the clue.

⟹ A 3 in a grid corner is the simplest case — the boundary itself creates the exposed corner.
LEMMA 1b

Exposed corner of a one

If a corner vertex of a 1-cell has no edges other than the two belonging to the cell, both edges at that corner are eliminated. Vertex parity requires both or neither — but if both were in the loop, the cell would have 2 edges, violating the clue.

LEMMA 2

Row of threes

In a row of adjacent 3 cells, every shared edge between them is in the loop, and the two outer edges parallel to the shared edges (top and bottom of the first and last cell) are also forced.

⟹ This extends to any number of consecutive 3s in a line.
LEMMA 2b

Zigzag threes

A fully-solved row of 3s forms a zigzag: each cell's missing edge alternates between top and bottom. The loop weaves through the shared edges like a circuit-board trace, entering from one side and exiting the other in a single continuous path.

LEMMA 3

Diagonal threes

Two 3 cells sharing a vertex (diagonally adjacent) force both edges of the shared vertex that don't border either 3-cell, since the shared vertex must have exactly 2 loop edges and both 3-cells demand edges there.

LEMMA 4

One loop to rule them all

An edge that would close the loop before all clues are satisfied must be eliminated. The loop must visit enough vertices to satisfy every clue.

LEMMA 5

Cascading chevrons

If two loop edges meet at a corner of a 2-cell, the cell's remaining two edges must meet at the opposite corner — forming a chevron. When that opposite corner is shared with another 2-cell, the pattern cascades diagonally.

⟹ A diagonal chain of 2s with a chevron at one end forces chevrons all the way through.
LEMMA 6

Trapped approach on a one

If a loop edge approaches a corner of a 1-cell and has no way to leave that vertex without entering the cell, the line must use exactly one of the two cell edges at that corner. This satisfies the clue, so the two edges at the opposite corner are eliminated.

LEMMA 7

Approaching a two

If a 2-cell has one edge eliminated and a loop edge approaches a vertex that borders two of the cell's three remaining edges, the third remaining edge (not at that vertex) is forced. The approaching line must use exactly one of the two edges at that vertex, accounting for one of the cell's two required edges — so the distant edge must supply the other.

Axioms — basic operations
AXIOM 1

Zero elimination

A cell with clue 0 has no loop edges — all four of its edges are immediately eliminated.

AXIOM 2

Clue satisfaction

If a cell already has N edges in the loop matching its clue N, all remaining edges of that cell are eliminated.

AXIOM 3

Remaining forced

If a cell with clue N has exactly N undetermined edges remaining (and the rest are eliminated), all remaining edges must be in the loop.

AXIOM 4

Vertex parity

Every vertex has 0 or 2 loop edges. If a vertex already has 2 loop edges, all other edges at that vertex are eliminated. If it has 1 loop edge and only 1 undetermined edge remains, that edge is forced.

AXIOM 5

No junctions

If two loop edges pass straight through a vertex, the perpendicular edges at that vertex must be eliminated — the loop cannot branch.

AXIOM 6

Dead end

If a vertex has only one undetermined edge and no loop edges, that last edge must be eliminated (a single edge cannot form a loop).

Glossary

Clear
An edge whose status is undetermined — it may or may not be part of the loop. Drawn as a faint gray line.
Eliminated
An edge confirmed to NOT be part of the loop. Shown with a red ✕ mark or removed entirely from the grid.
Exposed
A vertex of a cell where all non-cell edges have been eliminated or don't exist, leaving only the cell's own edges. An exposed corner of a 3-cell forces both edges.
Filled
An edge confirmed to be part of the loop. Drawn as a solid black line.
Forced
An edge that must be part of the loop, as determined by logical deduction. Shown in goldenrod to highlight the deduction.
Tail
A string of connected loop edges that attaches to the rest of the loop at only one end. The free end must eventually connect — it cannot dead-end.
